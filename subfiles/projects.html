Photography ● Photoshop ● Video ● 360 Video




Photography:



This selection of portrait photographs is a showcase of my early DSLR camera work. As an assignment for UIUC’s MACS 150 (Digital Media Production) course, this collection was produced to give a glance into the character of my partner. Cool, calm, and observant, my partner is presented through the various shots, settings, and stagings strategically selected by myself.



Photoshop:


Another project made for UIUC’s MACS 150 (Digital Media Production) course, this TV poster is a showcase of my early Photoshop work. The assignment required producing a TV poster for a fake show starring myself, using a portrait photograph of myself, a set of external images repurposed and reproduced under fair use, and Photoshop’s many tools to paint a picture of what my show would be. Catch Robbie Sieczkowski’s latest HBO drama, User One: The VR Tales, tonight at 9!



Video:


A MACS 100 (Intro to Popular TV and Movies) production, this project is my directorial debut. Featuring the tremendous talent that UIUC’s intro MACS-level classes have to offer, the production was a fun project where I got to dip my toes in all areas of pre-production, production, and post-production of short narrative video content.



360 Video:


My final project for UIUC's JOUR 460 (Extended Realities and Immersive Technologies) course, in this 360 video, my group set out to take prospective UIUC students on an immersive mini campus tour. Click on the video and move it around with your mouse. Or if you want to be really immersed, open the video in full screen on your phone and plug it into a Google Cardboard or Samsung Gear!
